The shima-enaga is a small, fluffy bird native to Hokkaido, Japan, known for its all-white head and face, which gives it the nickname "Japanese snow fairy". It is a subspecies of the long-tailed tit and is about 14 cm long, including its long tail. Due to its round shape and fluffy feathers, especially in winter, it is often described as looking like a ball of cotton.

Appearance:

The shima-enaga has a completely white face and body, unlike other long-tailed tit subspecies which have black streaks on their heads. Its overall appearance is small and round, and it can fluff its feathers in the cold to stay warm, enhancing its "fluffy" look.

Habitat and behavior:

They are found in forests, mountains, and city parks in Hokkaido. They live in flocks to huddle for warmth during winter and feed on insects and tree sap.

Cultural significance:

The bird has become a popular regional symbol and is often featured in local art, merchandise, and photography.

Interesting Fun Facts

  • Shima-enagas huddle together in winter.
  • They move very fast, making photos hard to take.
  • They live only in Hokkaido.
  1. They molt their feathers once a year.
  2. They travel in flocks for safety.
  3. Their nests can contain over 1,000 feathers.
